University Overview

Walter Sisulu University is a higher education institution in the Eastern Cape Province of South Africa. It was formed in 2005 and has positioned itself as a technology-infused African university with a strong commitment to academic excellence.

The university serves a large student population across campuses in Mthatha, Butterworth, Buffalo City, and Komani. It is especially important in supporting rural development, widening access, and responding to social and national needs through education.

  • Faculty of Engineering, Built Environment and Information Technology
  • Faculty of Medicine and Health Sciences
  • Faculty of Management and Public Administration Sciences
  • Faculty of Education
  • Faculty of Law, Humanities and Social Sciences
  • Faculty of Economics and Financial Sciences
  • Faculty of Natural Sciences

Details on WSU

Walter Sisulu University is named after the late Walter Max Ulyate Sisulu, one of South Africa's most respected liberation figures. The institution plays a powerful role in renewal, rural development, and access to meaningful higher education.

Established 2005
Students 27,500+
Programmes 186+
Employees 1,800
Location Eastern Cape
Partnerships 175+ Worldwide

Vision & Mission

Vision: An impactful, technology-infused African University.

Mission: Through its core business, Walter Sisulu responds to societal needs in ethical, scholarly, sustainable, and entrepreneurial ways, and delivers future-ready graduates.

  • Producing relevant, innovative, and impactful research
  • Championing sustainable and just development
  • Graduating versatile individuals
  • Promoting social justice and sustainable growth
  • Living core values such as honesty, integrity, quality, respect, and ubuntu

Milestones

  • WSU is a major economic player with assets of over R5 billion and an annual budget of over R1 billion.
  • All academic programmes are fully accredited by the Council for Higher Education.
  • Engineering programmes are accredited by the Engineering Council of South Africa.
  • The medical school is among the top 8 medical faculties in the world for Problem-based Community Learning.
  • WSU is accredited with SAICA to produce chartered accountants.
  • It produced the first cohort of BSc Clinical Associates to support rural health services.
  • The journalism school offers South Africa's only programme in Versatile Broadcasting.
  • WSU was the first of nine universities chosen to house an e-skills Knowledge Centre Network Hub.

Graduates & Alumni

WSU has produced and honoured many important figures in public life, science, journalism, law, and sport.

  • Mrs Albertina N. Sisulu, Doctor of Philosophy (Sociology 1992)
  • Dr Nkosazana C. Dlamini-Zuma, Doctor of Medicine
  • Chriselda Lewis, journalist
  • Mandisa Williams, female Springbok rugby captain
  • Dr Terence Nombembe, former Auditor-General
  • Judge Mbuyiseli Madlanga, constitutional judge
  • Nonkululeko Gobodo, one of the first Black chartered accountants to graduate
  • Dr Ncumisa Jilata, Africa's youngest neurosurgeon
